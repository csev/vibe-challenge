# vibe-challenge

This is a challenge for those who think that vibe coding is the future and there is no reason to learn anything when
you have ChaptGPT.  It was not designed to be easy or hard - it was just a real thing that happenned.

TL;DR; Dr. Chuck finished the challenge in less than 30 hours from looking at the exact specifications you are given
below to being in production and successfully supporing a three-ball tournament in Mike's bar.

The Back Story
--------------

Mike owns a pub and they wanted to do a memorial three-ball challenge for a veteran that passed away.  Mike
and Dr. Chuck play pickup hockey every Thursday night in East Lansing, MI.  On March 5, 2026 before the
hockey game started, Mike came over to Chuck at center ice and asked Chuck to write a desktop
application to run a three-ball https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three-ball pool tournament for
tournament on March 15.

At center ice that night, Chuck said a web based application with multiple screens would be smarter and
he would try to do it vibe-style if he had time.  Dr. Chuck asked Mike to write down how the game should
work.

Mike sent two hand-written sheets of paper with the rules of the game and an Excel spread sheet to
mock up a user interface. 

Chuck was busy and waited until Saturday March 14, 2026 to start coding.  He started in the lobby of the 
Frasier Big Boy hockey arena while his son Brent was playing wheelchair floor hockey.  An hour later
the first version of the application was done and online in production.  Before they left Frasier, MI
Chuck sent Mike an email with the URL to test.

After a few iterations Saturday and Sunday the software made its debut, Sunday March 15, 2026 and ran
the tournament.

The Challenge
-------------

Yuo are to take the three specifications in this repo and within 24 hours deliver a working application.
You get nothing else.

It does not have to be perfect - it has to work well enough for it to be used and given to the customer
for evaluation.   It has to be complete, and it has to work.  It does not have to be perfect.  You don't 
have access to Mike for him to react to improve the code.

The Rules
---------

If you are already a strong developer, you should be able to match Dr. Chuck's 24 hour result and perhaps
do a better job that Dr. Chuck.  That won't impress me much - AI should make you a better developer and
accellerate your productivity.

If you have no software development experience - except a one hour "Intro to VIBE Coding" Seminar, that is the
real test.  I expect this will be super painful and teach the lesson about just how foolish it is to pretend
that AI can substitute for a person who knows something about programming.
